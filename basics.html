Basics

Primary

In traditional colour theory and painting, red, yellow and blue are primary colours which are capable of forming most other colours through mixing with one another.

Tint

A tint is a mixture of a colour with white, increasing its lightness and obtaining a brighter result.

Monochromatic

A monochromatic colour scheme is comprised of colours of the same hue, extended with tints, shades and tones.

Secondary

Formed by mixing two primary colours together evenly, orange, green and purple are regarded as secondary colours.

Shade

A shade is a mixture of a colour with black, decreasing its lightness and obtaining a darker result.

Complementary

A complementary colour scheme is made out of two hues directly opposite from each other on the colour wheel.

Tertiary

Tertiary colours are formed by mixing two secondary colours together by an equal amount, granting access to more hues of colour.

Tone

A tone is a mixture of a colour with grey, or by both tinting and shading, creating a duller result.

Triadic

A triadic colour scheme is achieved with three hues evenly spaced from each other on the colour wheel, forming a triangle.
